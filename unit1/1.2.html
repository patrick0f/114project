Lesson 1.2 Critical Thinking

Hindsight bias-

The “I knew it all phenomenon”. We believe after learning about an outcome that we would have foreseen it.


Overconfidence-

People tend to know more than they think they do


Pseudoscience-

Popular beliefs seemingly related to science


Confirmation bias-

Looking for evidence to confirm beliefs while ignoring all other disproving evidence. Can be intentional or unintentional.


Scientific attitude-

An attitude composed of curiosity, skepticism, and humility.


Critical thinking-

Not blindly believing of arguments and conclusions. Examines assumptions, discerns values, evaluates evidence.


Scientific method-

Psychologists use the scientific method to construct theories that interpret observations. It is systematic, so that things can be proven or disproven.


Theory-

An explanation that integrates principles, organizes and predicts behaviors. Is highly researched and has tested frameworks that organize studies under an umbrella.


Hypothesis-

A testable prediction induced by theory that allows for revision or confirmation. Also a statement of relationship between variables. (If, then)


Research observation-

The application of a hypothesis through systematic observation and manipulation of variables.


Operational definition-

A precise definition of a variable being observed so that it is measurable and manageable (doable). Allows for scientists to replicate similar studies to prove or disprove the theory.
