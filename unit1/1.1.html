Lesson 1.1 Introduction to Psychology

Philosophers and Psychology-

The Hebrews, Aristotle, and Augustine thought that the mind and body are connected. Socrates, Plato, and Descartes thought that the mind and body are distinct from each other.

Socrates and Plato think that some ideas are inborn. Aristotle and John Locke think that the mind is a blank slate.


Dorothea Dix-

The first advocate for those suffering from mental illness and insisted they get help. Created the first mental asylum during the American Civil War.


Structuralism-

A major school of thought in psychology. Studies the consciousness, how the mind is organized (structured) and how its elements relate to one another.


Wilhelm Wundt-

Is considered the father of psychology since he established the first formal psychological lab in 1879. He used introspection, looking inward to consider decisions and thinking.


Functionalism-

Another major school of thought.


William James-

The first American psychologist. Criticized Wundt for his narrow ideas. Influenced by Darwin, he wanted to understand how consciousness, thoughts, and feelings work (function) to help people adapt in their environments. He used introspection, but also questionnaires and tests to truly see what people thought.


Gestalt Psychology-

Founded as a revolt against Wundt by Max Wertheimer, it believed that consciousness was best understood by observing the whole experience rather than breaking it down into various elements. “The whole is greater than the sum of its parts.”


Psychoanalysis-

Sigmund Freud focused on abnormal behavior. He believed all behavior and mental processes were influenced by unconscious mind and forces and that problems came from unresolved conflicts in the unconscious mind. He used free association, saying what comes to mind without censorship, and dream analysis to understand the mind. His ideas are controversial but still hold a lot of influence on psychology.


Behaviorism-

Became popular in the 1920s-1960s. B.F. Skinner and JB Watson disagreed with a lot of the field and believed that psychology should only study what can be observed and measured objectively (Watson).

Skinner insisted that external factors shaped behavior, that your thoughts and hidden unconscious parts of the mind are irrelevant.


Contemporary Psychology-

Psychology is defined as the study of behavior, what we do, and mental processes, inner thoughts and feelings.

There is controversy over if our traits and behaviors are based on biology (nature) or our environment (nurture).


Biopsychosocial approach-

Three influences that come together to influence behavior and the mind.

Biological influences include genetic predispositions, genetic mutations, natural selection of physiology and behaviors, and genes responding to the environment.

Psychological influences include learned fears and expectations, emotional responses, cognitive processing, and perceptual interpretations.

Socio-cultural influences include the presence of others, cultural, societal, and familial expectations, peer influences, and compelling models, like those of media.


Perspectives-

Biological perspective focuses on understanding how the brain and body physically creates thought, emotions, and memories. It asks how are messages transmitted through the body or how blood chemistry is linked with motives. Biological perspective is purely physical processes and anatomy in the body and mind.


Evolutionary perspective focuses on how natural selection had caused behavior and genes to develop. It asks how evolution influences behavior tendencies. Evolutionary perspective is about natural selection, reproduction, and spreading the gene pool.


Behavior genetics focuses on how our genes and environment influences individual differences. It asks to what extent does our genes or environment impact our personality. Behavior genetics is about behavior, environment, and how genes predispose behavior.


Psychodynamic (new psychoanalysis) focuses on the unconscious mind as the director of behavior. It asks how does our unconscious energy motivate us.


Behavioral perspective focuses on how our behavior is shaped by our learning process. It asks how we learn to fear things, how do we change certain behaviors. Behavioral perspective is about learning processes and learning.


Cognitive perspective is about how we process information and how our perceptions influence actions. It asks how we use info to remember and how our interpretation impacts behavior.


Humanistic perspective focuses on human growth, potential, and self-concept. It asks how we make ourselves better people. Humanistic perspective is about free will, self-actualization, and full-potential.


Socio-cultural perspective focuses on human behavior interpreted in a social and cultural context. It asks how various groups are alike and different and how are social influences different across different cultures. Socio-cultural perspective is about family, neighbors, peers, race, and culture.


Psychological Domains

Psychology has 2 domains, basic and applied research. Basic research is doing research for the sake of others and the betterment of others. Different psychologists do different things.


  • Biological psychologists analyze the connections between the mind and body.

  • Developmental psychologists study how behavior and mental processes change over your life.

  • Cognitive psychologists study how we perceive, think, and solve things.

  • Personality psychologists investigate persistent traits. (nature vs nurture)

  • Social psychologists look at the ways people influence each other.

  • Psychometric psychologists look at the theory and technique of psychological measurement. Involved in any sort of testing, psychological or not.

  • Industrial-organization psychologists study workplace behavior.

  • Educational psychologists look at teaching and learning problems.

  • Counseling psychologists help people cope with academic, vocational, and marital challenges.

  • Clinical psychologists study, assess, and treat those with psychological disorders

  • Positive psychologists focus on positive events and influences in life.


Clinical vs Psychiatry

Clinical psychologists have a PHD. Studies, assesses, and treats people with psychotherapy.

Psychiatrists are medical professionals (MD) that use drugs and psychotherapy to treat psychologically disease patients.

Human factors psychologists design machines that help assist perception.


American Psychological Association (APA)-

A scientific and professional organization of psychologists founded in 1892. Mary Calkins was the first female president, G. Stanley Hall was the first president and person to earn a PHD in psychology, and Margaret Floy was the first female to earn a psychology PHD.
