Lesson 1.6 Statistics

Statistical reasoning-

Statistical procedures analyze and interpret data.


Measures of tendency-

Mean, median, mode. Mode is the most frequently occurring score in a distribution.


Standard deviation is the average difference from each score to the mean.


A normal distribution creates a bell-shaped curve. The mean, median, and mode are at the exact same point.


A skewed distribution has outliers that can skew the data. A negative skew has a outlier on the left side, a positive skew has an outlier on the right side. The mean is the most impacted by outliers.


Inferential statistics estimates what is happening in a sample to make predictions about the rest of the population. There is a chance for error, so the hypothesis should be tested for significance.


Null hypothesis states that there is no difference between two sets of data. Until the research shows by proving the hypothesis that there is a difference, any difference shown must be attributed to chance. A null is essentially the counterpart/opposite of the hypothesis.


A type 1 error is when the null is rejected but it is actually true, a type 2 error is when the null is accepted but it is actually false.


Statistical significance-

The difference between two groups, the experimental and control, is not due to chance. It is due to an actual difference, the independent variable. Data is significant when the likelihood of difference due to chance is less than 5% or a 95% chance of difference due to independent variable.

Other Important Vocabulary

Empiricism-

Theory that all knowledge comes from experience and the senses


Cohort sequential study-

An experimental design where multiple measures are taken over time from two or more age groups.


Cross sectional study-

An experimental design where one measure is taken from many people at one point in time.
