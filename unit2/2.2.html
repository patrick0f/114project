Lesson 2.2 The Endocrine System


Endocrine system-

The body’s chemical communication system that releases hormones into the blood stream. It acts as the counterpart to the nervous system. Works with the parasympathetic nervous system to sustain homeostasis. Works to support the sympathetic nervous system in a crisis.


The endocrine system can release adrenaline (epinephrine) into the bloodstream from the adrenal glands to sustain the body’s defensive reaction/fight or flight response. The systems work together and the endocrine system finishes what the nervous system starts.


Pituitary gland-

The master gland of the endocrine system. Regulates activity of all other glands. Under the control of the hypothalamus and sends out hormone signals to other glands.


Thyroid (throat)-

Regulates metabolism, growth and development, and calcium rate


Adrenal gland (near kidneys)-

Regulates the fight or flight response and metabolism


Pancreas-

Regulates blood sugar in blood


Gonads-

Regulates body development of secondary sexual organs and maintains reproductive organs like sperm and eggs. Also produces testosterone and estrogen/progesterone.
