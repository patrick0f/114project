Lesson 2.3 The Nervous System


Nervous System-

Consists of all the nerve cells in the body. It is the body’s electro chemical communication system. It is composed of the central nervous system, containing the brain and spine, and the peripheral nervous system, containing the sensory and motor neurons that connects the brain and spine to everything else.


The brain is the body’s control center. The spine is the body’s way of transmitting messages to and from the brain.


Peripheral nervous system-

The peripheral nervous system has 2 parts.


The autonomic nervous system controls involuntary or automatic functions that happen automatically in the body like breathing and digestion.


The somatic nervous system (soma=body) controls the voluntary movements and communicates between the sensory organs.


Autonomic nervous system-

The autonomic nervous system has 2 parts.


The sympathetic nervous system controls physical arousal in the body, preparing it for stressful situation, expending energy, and initiating fight or flight. Increases heartbeat and oxygenated blood, dilates pupils, inhibits digestion, stimulates liver glucose release, and stimulates epinephrine and norepinephrine release through the adrenal gland (part of the endocrine system).


The parasympathetic nervous system calms the body conserves energy, and returns to homeostasis. Stimulates digestion, contracts pupils, and slows breathing and heart rate.


Types of Neurons-

Sensory neurons (afferent/affect) carry incoming information from the sensory receptors to the CNS (spine and brain).


Interneurons are the neurons in the Central nervous system and act as messengers between sensory and motor neurons. They help with reflexes.


Motor neurons (efferent) carry outgoing info from the CNS to the peripheral nervous system.


Reflex-

Any of a number of automatic, unlearned, relatively fixed responses to stimuli that do not require conscious effort and that often involve a faster response than might be possible if a conscious evaluation of the input were required.
