Lesson 3.1 Basics of Sensation and Perception

Sensation-

Detecting physical stimulus from the environment and converting it to neural signals


Perception-

Selecting, organizing, and interpreting sensations


Bottom-up processing-

The analysis of the stimulus begins with sense receptors and working up to the brain.


Top-down processing-

Information processing guided by higher level mental processes using experience and expectations


Absolute threshold-

The minimum stimulation needed to detect a stimulus 50% of the time


Difference threshold—

The minimum difference between two existing stimuli required for detection 50% of the time, just noticeable difference


Weber’s law-

The size of the difference threshold is proportional to the intensity of the stimulus. If the intensity is high, then the difference needed will be large and vice versa.


Subliminal threshold-

When stimuli are below the absolute threshold for awareness. Most will not detect it, but some will since it still exists. Subliminal words can prime a person’s responses but not impact a group’s habits.


Signal detection theory-

Predicts how and when we detect the presence of a faint stimuli amid background noise. Assumes detection is based on experience, expectations, motivation, and fatigue. Like hearing a voice in the middle of a crowd.


Sensory adaptation-

Diminished sensitivity as a result of constant stimulation
