Lesson 3.6 Interpretation

Restored vision-

After cataract surgery blind adults were able to see. They could differentiate figure and ground but could tell the difference between a circle and triangle.


Facial recognition-

Blind people, after regaining sight were unable to recognize faces, only distinct features. Normal people have a hard time when the lower half of a picture is changed in facial recognition.


Sensory deprivation-

Kittens raised without exposure to horizontal lines had later difficulty in perceiving horizontal bars. Suggests vision is a partly acquired sense. Sensations are impacted by a lack of stimulation.


Perceptual adaptation-

The visual ability to adjust to an artificially displaced field.


Perceptual set-

A mental predisposition to perceive one thing and not another.


Schema-

Concepts that organize and interpret unfamiliar information. Develop through experience.


Context effects-

A given stimulus can evoke different perceptions based on the context of the stimulus


Cultural context-

Context from culture can influence perception


Perception has biopsychosocial influences.


Biological influences include entry level sensory analysis, unlearned visual phenomena, critical period for sensory development.


Psychological influences included selective attention, learned schemas, gestalt principles, emotional context effects, and perceptual sets.


Socio-cultural influences include cultural assumptions and expectations and physical context effects.


Human factors-

Understanding human factors can help to design equipment that avoids disaster. Many accidents are based on errors of perception.


Extrasensory perception (ESP)-

Perception without sensory input. A large percentage of scientists don’t believe in ESP.


Paranormal phenomena include astrological predictions, healing, communication with the dead, out of body experiences, etc. The most relevant are telepathy, clairvoyance, and precognition.


Telepathy-

Mind to mind communication, one person sending thoughts and another receiving them.


Clairvoyance-

Perception of remote present events.


Precognition-

Perceiving future events.


Psychokinesis-

Performing a physical task with your mind, like telekinesis.


People continue to believe in ESP because they want to or because they know someone who thinks they have experienced ESP.


Scientists cannot validate ESP because they cannot replicate the instances of the abilities in labs.


Other Important Vocabulary

Accommodation-

The process of the eye’s lens changing shape to focus on objects near or far


Selective attention-

The focusing of conscious awareness on a stimulus


Change blindness-

Failing to notice changes in the environment


Inattentional blindness-

Failing to see visible objects when concentration is directed elsewhere


Visual cliff-

A lab device for testing depth perception in infants and young animals


Hypnagogic sensation-

An imagined sensation that feels real.
