Lesson 8.7 Somatic Symptom Disorders

Somatic symptom disorder-

Characterized by physical symptoms that suggest a physical disorder, but have no physical causes or evidence suggests that the symptoms are linked to psychological factors.


Conversion disorder-

Also called functional neurological symptom disorder, it is unexplained symptoms that affect voluntary motor or sensory function suggesting a medical condition that can’t be found. The onset is usually after a stressful event and common symptoms include paralysis, deafness, and difficulty walking. Many of the symptoms are anatomically impossible, like glove anesthesia, which is the inability to feel the hand, but sensation in the arm still exists.


Conversion episodes are triggered by stressful events or emotional conflicts.


La Belle indifference (beautiful indifference)-

A person with extreme anxiety about life, but a lack of concern about their incapacitating physical ailment. These symptoms allow them to escape from current events like frustrating events and can lead to a secondary gain like affection.


Factitious disorders-

False, or feigned disorders. Munchausen’s syndrome, Munchausen’s syndrome by proxy, and Ganser syndrome are factitious disorders.


Munchausen’s syndrome-

The intense preoccupation with making oneself ill. Most often, someone will physically impair themselves in some way.


Munchausen’s syndrome by proxy is when someone has a preoccupation with making others in proximity ill. Mothers will make their children ill, for example.


Illness anxiety disorder/hypochondriasis-

Preoccupation with the idea that one might get a serious disease, along with misinterpretation of bodily systems/functions.


The disease is marked by physiological arousal of worry, anxiety, and sleep disturbance.


Those with the disorder avoid others who might be ill and repeatedly check themselves for physical illness, such as engaging in repeated consultations with a medical professional.


Personality, life experiences, upbringing, and inherited traits all play a role in the disorder.


Bodily focus-

The close monitoring of body features and the focus on physical complaints.
