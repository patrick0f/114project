Lesson 8.9 Psychotherapy

Mental health specialists provide the largest outpatient psychotherapy.


Outpatient-

Patients do not live or stay overnight at a medical facility.


Psychotherapy-

Cognitive therapies are most widely used, with psychodynamic/analytic coming in a close second.


Clients can overestimate the effectiveness of therapy. Some enter therapy in crisis, which can subside over time in regression normalcy. Some believe that it has to be worth the effort, and others just speak kindly of their therapist.


Research does show treated patients were 80% better than untreated ones.


Disorders and therapies-

Treating depression, behavior, cognition, and interpersonal therapy can be used.


Treating anxiety, cognition, exposure, and stress inoculation therapy can be used.


To treat bulimia, cognitive behavioral therapy can be used.


Treating phobias, behavior therapy and exposure therapy can be used.


Alternative psychotherapies-

Therapeutic touch is when therapists move their hands a few inches from the patient’s body to push energy fields into balance, which doesn’t seem to work.


Eye movement desensitization and reprocessing, EMDR, is when people move their eyes to remember traumatic experiences.


Light exposure therapy has been found to treat seasonal affective disorder.


Clinical psychologist-

Experts with mostly PhDs in research, assessment, and therapy that is supplemented by internships.


Psychiatrists-

Physicians specializing in treating psychological disorders can prescribe medications.


Other Important Vocabulary

Somatoform disorder-

A disorder where the symptoms take a somatic/bodily form without any apparent cause.


Post traumatic growth-

Positive psychological changes that occur as a result of experiencing PTSD.


Poverty-

A risk factor correlated with disorders.


Virtual reality exposure therapy-

Anxiety treatment that progressively exposes people to simulations of their greatest fear.


Meta analysis-

A procedure for statistically combining the results of many research studies.


Repetitive transcranial magnetic stimulation-

The application of pulses of magnetic energy to the brain to stimulate or suppress activity.


Resilience-

Personal strength that helps people cope with stress and recover from adversity.


Insight therapy-

Therapy that improves psychological functioning by increasing the client’s awareness of underlying motives and defenses.


Client centered therapy-

A humanistic therapy by Carl Rogers where therapists use techniques like active listening to facilitate client growth.


Joseph Wolpe-

A pioneer of cognitive behavior therapy that dealt with PTSD veterans.


Stress inoculation therapy-

A form of cognitive behavior therapy for PTSD.


Rebirthing therapy-

A therapy using continuous focused breathing under the guidance of a practitioner. Generally considered pseudoscience by most researchers.


Regression towards the mean-

Following an extreme event, following events ae less likely to be extreme.
