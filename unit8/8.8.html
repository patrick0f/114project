Lesson 8.8 Treatment of Psychological Disorders

History of treatment-

The treatment of insane people in ancient times was based on irrational views, and people were subjected to strange and dangerous treatments. Phillipe Pinel and Dorathea Dix founded humane movements to care for mentally ill people.


Psychotherapy-

Involves an emotional, confiding interaction between a therapist and a mental patient. There are over 250 types of psychotherapy and therapists use eclectic mixes.


Biomedical therapy-

Uses drugs that act on the nervous system to cure psychological disorders.


Eclectic approach-

Uses healing techniques that vary depending on the client.


Psychoanalysis-

The first formal psychotherapy developed by Freud. He used the famous couch, where patients lay and say whatever comes to mind using free association. A therapist’s role is to provide insight and interpret the patient’s hidden conflicts.


The aim is to bring repressed feelings into conscious awareness so patients can deal with them. Thus, energy devoted to id-ego-superego conflict is lessened and so is anxiety.


Resistance-

The editing of thoughts to resist feelings and express emotions during free association. The hesitation shown when asked about something. Important in the analysis of conflict driven anxiety.


Transference-

The transferring and opening up of innermost private thoughts to a therapist and therefore developing positive or negative feelings towards them.


Criticisms-

Psychoanalysis is hard to refute because it can't be proven/disproven. It also takes a long time and can be expensive.


Interpersonal psychotherapy-

A variation of psychodynamic therapy that treats depression, focusing on symptom relief rather than overall personality change.


Humanistic therapy-

Carl Rogers aimed to boost self-fulfillment through helping grow self-awareness and self-acceptance.


Person centered therapy-

A form of humanistic therapy where the therapist listens to the needs of the patient and addresses their problems in a productive manner by building self-esteem.


Active listening-

The therapist echoes, restates, and clarifies the patient’s thoughts and acknowledges their feelings.


Behavioral/learning therapy-

Therapy that applies learning principles to eliminate unwanted behaviors. To treat things, therapists don’t delve beneath the surface to look for causes.


Changes maladaptive behavior and replaces it for appropriate behaviors. Treats most anxiety disorders.


Counterconditioning-

A classical conditioning procedure that conditions new responses to stimuli that trigger unwanted behaviors. Includes exposure therapy and aversive conditioning. Getting taught to cry and go fetal in response to a snake.


Exposure therapy-

Exposes patients to the things they fear. Repeated exposure lessens anxiety due to habituation to the fear.


Systematic desensitization-

A type of exposure therapy that associates a relaxed state with gradually increasing anxiety triggering stimuli used to treat phobias. Creates an association between a happy state and a phobia. Slow steps towards a phobia. You are shown an image of a snake, then it is brought near you, then you hold it.


Aversive conditioning-

A type of counterconditioning that associates unwanted states with unwanted behaviors. Temporary conditioned aversion to alcohol has been reported with this. Putting a bitter chemical on the nails to discourage biting them.


Operant conditioning-

Allows therapists to use behavior modification to reward desired behaviors and punish or not reward undesired behaviors. Withdrawn, uncommunicative autistic children have been trained by giving and withdrawing reinforcements for desired or undesired behaviors.


Token economy-

Creates a meaningless token used for privileges or treats that can be earned through exhibiting a certain behavior. Those in a psych ward are given 15 minutes of recess in exchange for keeping quiet for 5 minutes.


Cognitive therapy-

Teaches people adaptive ways of thinking based on the assumption that thought intervenes between events and emotional reactions. Helps get rid of bad, negative thoughts. You get fired and you believe you’re worthless, so you get depressed. You get fired and you believe you can do greater things, so you don’t get depressed.


Depression-

Aaron Beck and Aaron Ellis suggest that depressed patients don’t think they could ever be happy and thus associate minor fails, like failing a test, as major causes for depression. They believed that these thoughts needed to change for a patient to recover and used gentle questioning to do so.


Depressed patients were told to record daily positive events and show how they contributed to the events to display how they had control over positive things in life. These patients showed lower depression scores compared to other depressed patients.


Cognitive behavior therapy-

Combines the reversal of negative thoughts with the modification of behavior. Aims to alter the way people act, behavior therapy, and the way they think, cognitive therapy.


Rational emotive behavior therapy

Helps individuals see their false ways of thinking in a direct and confrontational way. The most controversial types of therapies because it tells people that they are wrong, and they should change.


Group therapy-

Consist of 6-9 people in a session so more people can be helped for a lower cost. Clients benefit from the knowledge that others have similar problems as them.


Family therapy-

A family together working with a clinician to improve communication and resolve conflicts.


Antipsychotic drugs-

Comes in chlorpromazine, or Thorazine, clozapine, or Clozaril, Seroquel, Risperdal, and Zyprexa, which block serotonin and dopamine. Most of these medicines/drugs block dopamine receptors, working as antagonists. Side effects include sluggishness, tics/involuntary body movements (tardive dyskinesia), lowering of white blood cells, and weight gain. Treats schizophrenia.


Tardive dyskinesia-

Mild to severe twitching, shaking, or jerking in the body.


Antimanic drugs-

Comes in lithium, or lithium carbonate, anticonvulsant meds, and Depakote. These medicines reduce manic activity. Doses have to be controlled because lithium is powerful and can easily become toxic. Treats bipolar disorder. Bipolar lithium.


Antidepressant drugs-

Comes in SSRIs such as Prozac, Zoloft, celexa, and Lexapro, or SNRIs such as Effexor and Cymbalta. SSRIs block reuptake of serotonin and SNRIs block reuptake of serotonin and norepinephrine. Side effects include dry mouth, constipation, headaches, and weight gain.


Antianxiety drugs-

Comes in benzodiazepines including Librium, valium, Ativan, and Xanax. These medicines work to reduce the anxiety of responses to stimuli that signal punishment and frustration. Used for a short time due to dependency risks. Side effects include drowsiness, reduced concentration, lethargy, and physical dependence. Treats OCD, GAD, phobias, etc.


ECT (electroconvulsive therapy)-

Used to treat severe depression by passing currents through the brain using 2 electrodes. Resets the hypothalamus and readjusts hormone levels.


Psychosurgery-

A lobotomy where the nerves between the frontal lobe and center of inner brain structures are severed. Side effects include permanent lethargy, immaturity, and an impulsive personality.
